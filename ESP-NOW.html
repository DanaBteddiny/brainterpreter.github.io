ESP-NOW

The ESP-NOW Communication Protocol is a low-power, peer-to-peer wireless communication technology developed by Espressif Systems. Unlike traditional Wi-Fi communication, ESP-NOW allows multiple ESP32 devices to exchange data directly with each other without the need for a router or internet connection. This makes it ideal for low-latency applications that require fast, synchronized responses.

In the Brainterpreter project, ESP-NOW is used to wirelessly transmit commands from the ESP32 sender to multiple ESP32 smart plugs. This protocol ensures real-time control over smart appliances, enhancing the responsiveness of brainwave-driven automation. Its ability to connect up to 20 peers with minimal delay makes it a powerful choice for smart home ecosystems.

Specifications
  • Peer-to-Peer Communication:Direct messaging between ESP32 devices without the need for a central hub.
  • Ultra-Low Latency:Near-instantaneous data transmission for real-time control.
  • Low Power Consumption: Optimized for energy efficiency, perfect for smart home applications.
  • Supports up to 20 Peers:Enables wide scalability in automation networks.
  • Range of Up to 200 Meters: Reliable connectivity even in large spaces.
